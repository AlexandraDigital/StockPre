Reading Line Charts

A line chart connects the closing price of each period into a smooth curve. It's the simplest way to see the overall direction (trend) of a stock.

How to read trends:

  • 📈 Uptrend: The line makes higher highs and higher lows — bullish signal.
  • 📉 Downtrend: Lower highs and lower lows — bearish signal.
  • ➡️ Sideways: Price oscillates between a range — consolidation phase.

💡 Tip: Use the 1Y range to spot the big-picture trend before zooming into shorter timeframes for entry points.

Support & Resistance

Support is a price floor where buyers tend to step in (the line bounces up). Resistance is a ceiling where sellers push price down. When price breaks through resistance, that level often becomes new support — this is called a breakout.

Understanding Candlestick Charts

Each candlestick shows 4 prices for a time period: Open, High, Low, Close (OHLC).

  • 🟢 Green candle: Close > Open (buyers won the period)
  • 🔴 Red candle: Close < Open (sellers won)
  • Body: the thick block between Open and Close
  • Wicks/Shadows: thin lines showing High and Low extremes

Key Patterns

  • 🔨 Hammer: Long lower wick, small body at top — potential reversal from downtrend
  • ⭐ Doji: Open ≈ Close, equal wicks — indecision, watch for direction change
  • 🌟 Engulfing: A large candle completely "swallows" the previous one — strong reversal signal
  • 🌙 Shooting Star: Long upper wick, small body at bottom — bearish reversal at highs

💡 Tip: Never rely on a single candlestick pattern alone. Always confirm with volume and the overall trend before trading.

Volume Analysis

Volume is the number of shares traded in a period. It tells you how much conviction is behind a price move.

  • 📈 + 📊 Price up + high volume: Strong buying interest — bullish confirmation
  • 📉 + 📊 Price down + high volume: Strong selling pressure — bearish confirmation
  • 📈 + 📉 Price up + low volume: Weak move, could reverse — treat with caution
  • 📉 + 📉 Price down + low volume: Sellers aren't motivated — may be a temporary dip

Volume Spikes

A sudden spike in volume (2-3× the average) often signals a major event: earnings release, news, institutional buying, or the start of a new trend. Always check why volume is spiking.

💡 Tip: Volume is a leading indicator. Rising volume on up days and falling volume on down days is the hallmark of a healthy uptrend.

Key Metrics Explained

  • Market Cap: Total value of all shares (Price × Shares Outstanding). Classifies companies as Large Cap (>$10B), Mid Cap ($2-10B), Small Cap (<$2B).
  • P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings): How much you pay per $1 of earnings. A P/E of 20 means investors pay $20 for $1 of annual profit. Higher P/E = growth expectations; lower P/E = potentially undervalued or slow growth.
  • 52-Week Range: The highest and lowest prices over the past year. Helps gauge where the stock is relative to recent history.
  • Dividend Yield: Annual dividend payment as a % of stock price. A 3% yield on a $100 stock means $3/year in dividends.
  • Volume: Today's trading activity vs. average gives context for how notable the current move is.
  • Open / High / Low: Today's opening price, intraday high, and intraday low.

💡 Tip: Always compare the P/E ratio to the stock's industry peers. A tech company with P/E 40 might be reasonable; a utility company at P/E 40 would be expensive.

Common Investing Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ FOMO Buying: Chasing a stock after a huge run-up. The best gains are often already priced in.
  • ❌ Ignoring the trend: Trading against the prevailing trend (trying to catch falling knives).
  • ❌ No stop-loss: Letting losses run unchecked because you "believe" in the stock.
  • ❌ Overtrading: Trading too frequently based on short-term noise, racking up fees and taxes.
  • ❌ Ignoring volume: Taking a breakout seriously when it happens on very low volume.
  • ❌ Confirmation bias: Only reading news that supports your existing position.
  • ❌ Risking too much: Putting all your capital in one position. Diversification is key.

💡 Golden Rule: Cut losses quickly, let winners run. Never risk more than you can afford to lose.
